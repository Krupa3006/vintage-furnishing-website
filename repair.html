ASSESSMENT





Make a thorough inspection of the watch to ascertain its general health and spot any obvious defects. Make a note of the watch's make, model, and year of production because these details will be useful when it comes time to restore it.

DISASSEMBLY





First, take off the watch's strap or bracelet, if any. Using the proper tools, carefully remove the movement from the case by opening the case back. Before continuing with the disassembly, take pictures or make notes to remember the initial placement of each component.

CLEANING





To get rid of grime, oil, and outdated lubricants, clean each component individually using specialised watch cleaning solutions. Depending on the type of watch parts, use ultrasonic cleaners or manual cleaning methods. Use appropriate tools to handle the delicate components with care, and refrain from applying excessive force.

INSPECTION AND REPAIR





Check each component for signs of wear, corrosion, or damage. Any worn or damaged parts, such as rusted springs, broken jewels, or worn-out gears, should be replaced. Attend to any mechanical problems, such as inaccurate timekeeping, erratic winding, or a broken date complication. If necessary, polish or refinish the case and other external components.

LUBRICATION






To ensure smooth operation, lubricate the movement components as necessary. Use the appropriate amounts and locations of premium synthetic watch oils and greases. Avoid overlubricating because it can attract dust and lead to issues.

REASSEMBLY





Start putting the watch back together by cautiously returning each component to its original location. To ensure proper assembly, refer to the notes or reference photos taken during disassembly. Verify that each component fits snugly and functions easily.

REGULATION AND TESTING






To ensure precise timekeeping, adjust the watch's movement. Control the balance wheel's settings to ensure accurate oscillation. To ensure the watch's dependability and accuracy, test it in various settings and circumstances.

REFINISHING AND RESTORATION:





Polish the case, crystal, and other external parts to bring them back to their former luster. If the crystal is scratched or broken, replace it. If the dial or hands are beginning to show signs of wear, think about having them refinished, but only if absolutely necessary and after consulting a professional to protect the watch's value.

FINAL INSPECTION







Check the watch carefully for any remaining flaws or problems. Make sure that every part is correctly fastened and positioned. Check the watch's operation, making sure it can wind, set the time, and operate any complications.

REASSEMBLY AND FINAL TOUCHES






Reassemble the watch completely as soon as you are happy with the restoration. Refasten the bracelet or strap by making sure it is firmly fastened. Clean the watch's exterior to get rid of any dirt or fingerprints.
